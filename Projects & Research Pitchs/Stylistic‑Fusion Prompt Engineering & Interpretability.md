# Stylistic‑Fusion Prompt Engineering & Interpretability
*Blending Dostoevsky’s psychological torque with Lispector’s lyrical shimmer—then mapping the internals.*

> **Status** · proposal  
> **Lead** Tomás Pavan  
> **Tags** Prompt‑Engineering · Literary‑Fusion · Interpretability · Creativity

---

## 1 Executive Snapshot (≤120 words)
We extract author‑specific linguistic fingerprints and build a **Fusion‑Prompt Compiler** that interpolates them at controllable weights. Outputs are evaluated for coherence, originality, and affect—and, crucially, we trace which heads and neurons light up when stylistic vectors collide.

---

## 2 Research Questions
| Axis | Key Question |
|------|--------------|
| **Coherence vs. Novelty** | How far can vector interpolation go before meaning fractures? |
| **Head Localisation** | Do distinct “style heads” emerge for each author pool? |
| **User Resonance** | Which fusion ratios score highest on human affect scales? |

---

## 3 Method Blueprint

### 3.1 Fusion Pipeline
1. **Corpus Curation** — clean, balanced texts per author.  
2. **Feature Extraction** — embedding centroids + syntactic n‑gram stats.  
3. **Prompt Compilation** — YAML recipe specifying weight α (Author A) / β (Author B).  
4. **Generation & Scoring** — GPT‑4o runs + human blind‑ratings.  
5. **Interpretability Pass** — attention heat‑maps on style collision tokens.

---

## 4 Deliverables
- **Fusion‑Prompt Library** (Dostoevsky ⊕ Lispector, Borges ⊕ Lao Tzu, etc.).  
- **Style Collision Atlas** interactive head‑maps.  
- **Whitepaper** “Superposed Literary Vectors in Transformer Space.”  
- **CLI Tool** `fusion‑prompt compile --authorA lispector --authorB dostoevsky --alpha 0.4`.
